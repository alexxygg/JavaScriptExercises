String Templates

There are multiple ways to make templates for our strings.

Here we have a few variables, they represent different types of amounts and information.

let eggPrice = .20;

let eggsBought = 36;

let summary = "You bought " + eggsBought + " eggs for a total of $" + eggPrice * eggsBought + " dollars!"

RESULTS IN --> 'You bought 36 eggs for a total of $7.2 dollars!'

Template literals ${}

These templates make it easier to do operations through expressions, but they need back-ticks ```````````` when opening and closing the strings. (AltGr + }) and a dollar sign followed by opening and closing curly braces, where our math will be.

`Hello, you paid ${55 + 65} dollars.`

RESULTS IN --> 'Hello, you paid 120 dollars.'

This way, it will be evaluated in JS, if we use single or double quotes it will look like this:

"Hello, you paid ${55 + 65} dollars."

RESULTS IN --> 'Hello, you paid ${55 + 65} dollars.'

First template but with LITERALS.

Originally:

let summary = "You bought " + eggsBought + " eggs for a total of $" + eggPrice * eggsBought + " dollars!"

Now:

let summary = `You bought ${eggsBought} eggs for a total of $${eggPrice * eggsBought} dollars!`

BOTH RESULT IN --> 'You bought 36 eggs for a total of $7.2 dollars!'

We used a lot less quotes, and no plus signs to string it all together!

And if we wanted, we could use string methods inside the curly braces too.
